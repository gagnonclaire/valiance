As a player's character completes jobs they increase in character tier. Higher tier characters are more powerful, with access to a greater breadth of traits and abilities.
# From Aspirant to Valiant
After completing their first job, a player character increases their tier from 0 to 1. This marks their growth into a proper valiant, an adventuring mercenary capable of operating independently in the field.
## Archetypes
Upon reaching tier 1, a player must select an archetype for their character. The archetype itself offers no specific character features, but determines which classes and archetype features a character has access to.
### Archetype Classes
The primary features of an archetype are contained within its classes. Each archetype class contains 10 tiered features. When a player chooses a class at tier 1 they gain all of the traits and abilities associated with its tier 1 feature. These traits and abilities are foundational pieces of that class and often define its role within a team. 
### General Archetype Features
Every archetype has a list of general features with tier requirements ranging from 1 to 10. These feature contain traits and abilities that can be useful to any of the archetype's classes. When a player character increases their tier, they gain an archetype feature of their choice, permanently gaining the trait or ability tied to that feature. 
Upon reaching tier 1, a player may select any feature with a tier requirement of 1
# Progression Beyond Tier 1
Growth beyond tier 1 works similarly to the jump from tier 0 to tier 1, with characters gaining additional class and general features. 
## Higher Tier Archetype Features
Class feature progression is straightforward, with every tier earning the next class feature. General feature selection, however, allows for more creative progression decisions.
### General Archetype Features at Higher Tiers
When a player increases their tier beyond 1, they select any feature that they meet the tier requirement for, even if the tier of the feature is lower than their character tier. While foregoing higher tier features usually results in a weaker character, some combinations of lower tier features can allow for unique tactics.
## Advanced Progression Options
The following progression rules allow for far more variance in character progression. They give more experienced groups a chance to test the limits of the system, but doing so could detract form the experience of less veteran players. It's important to consult with the rest of the players before using any of these advanced progression options.
### Multiclassing Within Archetypes
The most straightforward of the advanced progression options is the allowance of multiclassing. When a character increases in tier the player may select any archetype class feature for that tier, rather than gaining the next feature for their original class.
This allows a more constrained expansion of character progression options, with players able to create hybrid characters capable of excelling in multiple roles. It could also lead to more complicated trait and ability interactions, which may or may not be desired by the group.
### Cross-Archetype Multiclassing
Cross-archetype multiclassing opens up the character progression space dramatically, which could lead to all manner of unintended feature interactions.  This option gives plenty of space for veteran players to stretch character progressions to its limits. 
Usually. this option is used alongside standard multiclassing rules, but it can be used on its own if desired. On group discretion, cross-archetype multiclassing can even be limited to a single class selection per archetype.
When using this type of multiclassing, the class features available to characters are limited. A player can only select class features from other archetype classes starting at character tier 3, and the selected features must be 2 tiers below the character tier. This allows truly hybrid characters with multiple foundational features, but limits the game changing tier 9 and 10 features to those within their own archetype.
### Cross-Archetype General Features
Similar to cross-archetype multiclassing, this progression option allows players to  gain general features from archetypes other than their own. It has the same tier limitation, limiting the selection to general features with tier requirements at least 2 tiers below the character tier. Like cross-archetype multiclassing, this can only be done starting at tier 3.
While less complex than cross-archetype multiclassing, this option should rarely be used on its own as most general features assume access to archetype class features.
